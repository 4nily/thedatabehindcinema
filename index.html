THE DATA
BEHIND
CINEMA


By Janen.

01

Cinema

Movies have been around for a long time. Over the years, people started tracking statistics regarding cinema, such as how long they are, how much they cost, and how many people go to watch them in the cinema. Looking at that information and graphs provided later on demonstrates some patterns in how cinema has significantly changed.

As shown by the blue bars in the graph above, ticket prices have gradually increased over time. However, when adjusting for inflation represented by the red line, tickets back then were actually more expensive than one might expect, especially from the 1970s and 80s. So while prices may appear they’ve gone up a lot, the real increase isn’t as significant.

02

Runtime

Back in the 1930s and 40s, movies were a bit shorter than they are today. After around the 1960s, most films settled into a range of about 100 to 110 minutes, and that hasn’t really changed much since.

The average movie length today, as of 2026, is about 114 minutes long.

03

Box Office

Box office revenue has increased over many decades due to an increase in ticket prices. A drop in 2020 is discernible as it was a time when theaters shut down due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Since then, revenue has recovered, but not entirely as it was before.

04

Attendance

Ticket sales were much higher early on, then slowly declined over time with the rise of TV and later streaming entertainment options such as Netflix, Disney+, Paramount, Sony, Max, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video, Apple TV+, etc. A big drop in 2020 is discernible as theaters closed during the COVID-19 pandemic. After the pandemic, ticket sales somewhat recovered, but they still haven’t fully returned to earlier levels.

05

Genre

Drama is the most common genre by a wide margin. Comedy comes next, while genres like documentary, sci-fi, and animation make up smaller portions overall. In general, most movies tend to fall into either the drama or the comedy category.

Letterboxd started as a film-tracking site and has grown into a larger platform. In 2016, people logged around 20 million film ratings, and by 2025 that number had risen to around 700 million. This difference underscores how watching movies has become an activity people actively log and review.
